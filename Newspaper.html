KIIT University Student's Suicide Sparks Protests, Arrest Made

Outrage erupts after tragic incident at Bhubaneswar campus

BHUBANESWAR - Tension gripped the campus of KIIT University on Thursday as students staged massive protests following the apparent suicide of a third year student. The incident has raised serious questions about campus mental health policies and hostel regulations.

The deceased, identified as 21-year-old computer science student Rajesh Mehta (name changed), was found unresponsive in his hostel room early Wednesday morning. While official autopsy reports are pending, preliminary investigations suggest death by hanging.

"This was not just a personal tragedy but an institutional failure," said protesting student leader Priya Sharma. "We've been demanding better counseling services and more humane hostel curfew rules for months."

Police confirmed the arrest of a senior university administrator in connection with the case. "We're investigating allegations of administrative negligence and mental harassment," said Bhubaneswar SSP R.K. Das.

The protest turned violent Thursday afternoon when demonstrators attempted to march to the Vice-Chancellor's residence. Security forces used water cannons to disperse the crowd after reports of property damage.

KIIT University officials released a statement expressing "deep sorrow" over the incident but denied allegations of institutional negligence. The university has announced an internal inquiry committee while classes remain suspended until Monday.
